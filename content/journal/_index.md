**International Transitions** is a peer-reviewed multidisciplinary journal dedicated to the study of major transformations affecting societies, organizations, institutions, and economies. It brings together contributions from economics, management, law, sociology, and international studies to examine the dynamics of change in a globalised and rapidly evolving world.

The journal publishes original research articles, review essays, and policy discussions. It welcomes both theoretical and empirical contributions that shed light on the mechanisms, drivers, and consequences of institutional transitions at all levels of analysis — from firms and higher education institutions to national economies and international systems.

---

## Scope and Topics

- International and geopolitical transitions
- Organizational transformation and adaptation
- Institutional change and regulatory reform
- Knowledge transfer and innovation ecosystems
- Migration, demographic transitions, and human capital
- Digital transformation and the economics of artificial intelligence
- Higher education and the futures of work
